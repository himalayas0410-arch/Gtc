ICAI–IFSCA Strategic Collaboration

Strengthening India's position as a global financial hub through institutional synergy and international financial services excellence at GIFT City.

MoU Active (2023-2026)

Strategic Partnership Overview

The Institute of Chartered Accountants of India (ICAI), through its Global Trade & Services Committee, established a landmark partnership with the International Financial Services Centres Authority (IFSCA) on 24 November 2023.

This collaboration focuses on promoting opportunities in international financial services, particularly at GIFT City, Gandhinagar, and building professional capacity to serve global markets.

MoU Exchange Ceremony

MoU signed during the GloPac Event in Ahmedabad, witnessing the formalization of cooperation between ICAI and IFSCA to foster growth in India's IFSC ecosystem.

"The ICAI–IFSCA collaboration continues to create awareness on GIFT City opportunities, build professional capacity, and enable global opportunities for Chartered Accountants."

Key Highlights 2025

Landmark Initiatives & Engagements

ICAI Delegation to GIFT City

On 28 August 2025, a high-level delegation of 63 members visited GIFT City. The visit included tours of NSE IX, Bank of Baroda (IBU), and DevX, providing deep insights into the IFSC ecosystem.

IFSCA Chintan Shivir

ICAI participated in the IFSCA Chintan Shivir roundtable on 11 September 2025. This engagement with industry stakeholders focused on shaping policies for professional services exports.

Policy Impact & Recognized Services

ICAI's proposal for the recognition of accounting and bookkeeping services under the IFSCA Act was accepted by the Government of India, resulting in formal notification on 18 January 2024.

About Global ICAI

The Global Trade & Services Committee of ICAI is committed to strengthening India’s presence in global services trade by promoting international collaboration, enhancing professional opportunities, and positioning accounting and finance as key export services in the evolving global economy. The Committee works closely with government bodies, industry stakeholders, and international institutions to facilitate trade integration, policy engagement, and global professional mobility.
